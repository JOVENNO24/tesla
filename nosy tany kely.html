NOSY TANY KELY

description:

Nosy Tanikely is a protected marine reserve 10km south of Nosy Be.
It's one of the best snorkelling sites in the area, with coral, numerous fish and sea turtles.
Snorkelling is best in the morning, before the wind picks up.
The Nosy Tanikely marine reserve, with its abundant coral reefs and exceptional biodiversity
provides habitats for numerous underwater species.
The warm tropical waters and alluvial deposits from the Sambirano River
mean that a large number of species have made this small islet their home.
Indeed, a varied and spectacular fauna lives all around the island.
Nosy Antanihely means “the island of small land”.
It is an uninhabited islet that is also of scenic interest to those less interested in the seabed.
Visitors can go there on day trips, but cannot sleep on the island
Access to the island has been controlled since 2010.
A 700m radius around the lighthouse marks the boundary of the nature reserve zone.
The infrastructure on site is very modest: a reception, a picnic area, an interpretation centre and toilets.
It is best spot for Snorkelling: Grab your flippers, mask and snorkel and enjoy the pleasures of snorkelling at Tanikely.
Observe the corals and underwater fauna from the surface or by snorkelling
With its soft sand and turquoise waters, Nosy Tanikely’s beaches are a paradise,
ideal for a pleasant swim or siesta under the vegetation, sheltered from the sun.
